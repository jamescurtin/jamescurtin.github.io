About Me

James W. Curtin

Who I am

I am a high achiever seeking to lend my skills to help others make life easier. I strive for efficiency and I am always on the lookout for more effective solution. I believe in the inherent value of teams and recognize the importance of organizing human capital in ways that maximizes the productivity of each member of the team. I look forward to the next project I can tackle!

Education and Experience

I am a student at Harvard College studying Government. I am a resident of Pforzheimer House and a member of the Harvard University Institute of Politics (IOP), where I serve on the Student Advisory Council. At the IOP, I lead the Policy Program–the largest IOP program, by membership. I administrated the production of multiple policy briefs commissioned by the Massachusetts State Senate on topics ranging from education reform to medical device regulation. I am pursuing a secondary (minor) in Computer Science, and am in the process of learning multiple programming languages. I am proficient in C, Python, PHP, and SQL, as well as HTML5 & JavaScript, and I am also in the process of learning Java.

I recently finished serving the communities of Bridgewater and Raynham, Massachusetts as an elected member of the Bridgewater-Raynham Regional School Committee. I was elected to a three-year term in 2012 at the age of 18—the youngest elected official in either town’s history. Serving my community was an honor for me, as it provided the opportunity to lend my time and effort to improve the school district that was so influential in contributing to my personal development.

I am a former White House Associate, where I worked in the Office of Presidential Correspondence (OPC). At Harvard University, I assist English language learners improve their skills and become more comfortable in conversational settings at the Bok Center for Teaching and Learning. I also work as a Student Software Developer for Harvard University Information Technology working with the Teaching & Learning Technologies devision.

I am an Eagle Scout and maintain membership with the Boy Scouts of America as a Troop Committee Member in my home troop. I enjoy the outdoors, camping, hiking, and exploring.


My Photography

I spent the summer of 2013 working at the Dumbarton Oaks Research Library in Washington, DC, which was ranked by National Geographic as having the 6th best public garden in the world. After spending a few weeks in the gardens during my free time, I decided to take up photography to document the beauty that I saw. Since then, my interest as grown and I strive to improve my skill as a photographer and capture meaningful moments in my life. I welcome the opportunity to do event photography or portraiture for individuals in the Harvard community or greater-Cambridge area. I currently shoot on a Nikon D700 and primarily use a 50mm f/1.8 Nikkor prime lens.
